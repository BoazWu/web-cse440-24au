When walking recreationally, it is difficult to find a route that is safe, 
easily navigable, and fulfills a set of preferences–especially when exploring. 
In contrast to someone who wants an efficient route from point A to point B, 
those walking for leisure may place higher value on avoiding distracting noises 
or on walking near pleasant scenery. Existing mapping technology that supports 
walking treats walking directions like a driving route, and as such is targeted 
only towards efficient walkers–therefore, the niche of recreational walking goes 
unsupported. Because of this, many recreational walkers feel they must stick to 
routes they know; whether that exploration be small ventures from existing routes 
or fully unexplored trails, the human need for exploration is left unmet.

Our design, BoredWalk, supports the niche of recreational walking by providing 
its users with unobtrusive walking directions. These directions guide recreational 
walkers along novel walking routes that match the user’s selected preferences. 
BoredWalk utilizes the spatial audio function that exists in modern headphones 
to deliver these directions, shifting between left, right, or center, depending 
on which direction the user needs to take.

## Design Walkthrough

An interesting aspect of our app design is the fact that the core functionality -
navigating a walker with audio feedback - needs no visual design, and minimal design
overall. As such, our design focus was on the ancillary features which support
the user.

This task is fundamental to our design, being the primary action that everyone 
will take when using our technology. Most other tasks did not make much sense 
unless this task was also included. This design was initially conceived to be 
an alternative to apps like Google Maps, and so we need to provide the same 
functionality. Participants in interviews wanted to go on new routes but did not 
want to risk getting lost or encountering unpleasant situations.

Unlike tradition mapping apps, BoredWalk presents routes which match a walker's
preferences and their desired time and distance. These can be point-to-point routings
or loops. While on the walk, the ambulist is free to deviate, and BoredWalk will
reroute as necessary.
